A natural progression has happened and more ambitious questions about web3 are being asked and domains are being pushed, however it's not ambitious enough. it's important to consider what decentralized science means.

Science today is limited buy 2 obvious hurdles of centralized institutions but there's a third and even more exciting avenue to consider. 

Funding is the obvious challenge. The sheer cost of doing science in the physical world is that only deep pockets that are capable of raising large sums of money can make significant contributions.

Second is information sharing among scientists (and public at-large), today it's ridden with heavy pay walls, fears of stolen ideas and intellectual dishonesty. That is resulting in science that is rarely repeated or repeatable, which is undermining the credibility of any of the science that gets published today. 

The third and little considered domain is the act of data collection and information processing. It's probably for good reason since the vast majority of current leaders in desci are coming from the very institutions they wish to replace, or they are coming from the blockchain industry and have a solution looking for a problem. There's also a major problem today in data collection which hasn't been linked up with desci at this time. It's getting quality data from the fringes.

Remote instruments, cheap apparatus for others to use, and data processing with verifiable algorithms and enhanced with cryptographic proof of authenticity and metadata locking the provenance down.

When we look at the transition from the analog world into a digital one, wet labs are limited because we don't have any way to trust that the data collection and entry were done in a credulous way. And lets be honest, we shouldn't trust just anyone to provide data which informs the most fundamental science discoveries. The scientific machine has gotten over this with the 'expert' approach. Put some people through an arduous process that only disciples dedicated to science would be willing to go through, use a flimsy peer review process, and publish to academic journals ran by corporations. I'm calling to shatter this old and outdated process of data collection and information processing. that's the fundamental problem which is preventing progress.

Rather than reshuffling the obvious changes that would benefit from a blockchain with global state, and simple smart contracts let us rethink what the purpose of these institutions truly are and build alternatives.
