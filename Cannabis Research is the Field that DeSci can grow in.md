Cannabis Research is the fertile Field in which DeSci can grow in

![[bd227abe-ea67-4fa5-b0ba-b9db07a4e773-0-3463479010.png]]

2022 is a special time where innovation is pushing power to the edges. The Federal Reserve is being replaced by Decentralized money, Wall Street is being replaced by Decentralized Finance (DeFi), and now ideas on how to Decentralize Science are scrambling for success. the core promises of the decentralized science movement is finding new ways to fund, replicate, and share science for an improved human condition. Through DeSci's promise, citizens will be closer to the results of science, the funding cycle of that science, and the scientists themselves.

Here, I'll share my thoughts on why I believe that cannabis research is a great place to test drive and quickly try new compositions of innovative DeSci tools.

 When science is funded, it's through old standing institutions with deep pockets, which spend millions of dollars without an obvious return on investment. There's basically three categories of funding to consider; Federal institutions, Industry, and non-profits. With a broad brush these groups are hamstrung by operating within federal laws,  and it's become practically impossible to do science on cannabis Jurisdictions where cannabis is legal have existed in the USA since 2012.

- Academic labs will not touch research which requires handling cannabis due to risk of losing federal funding
- Pharma labs won't touch cannabis because it's rather difficult to patent any products
- A clear social impact with people exists, and the populace deserves representation
- The cannabis community has a long tradition of grass roots based community support
- There are many low cost experiments that have high impact
- The scientific community is excited about learning more, however they won't risk their federal funding
- Cannabis isn't subject to national regulatory pressures, allowing for rapid funding cycles outside of FDA/DOE caterpillar pace
- Given that cannabis has been in the public domain for millenia, it's rather difficult to gain patents on there for pharma companies won't fund it's research

These combined factors allow for DeSci a fertile field to get it's feet wet with innovative new tools such as recording global state on blockchain, instrumental data with provenance and provable authenticity, and composability.

The majority of current DeSci work is in the trad pharmaceutical sphere, and I argue that DeSci shouldn't go directly against the massive institutions right away but start with a fertile envelope of opportunity using jurisdictional arbitrage. Big Pharma wont come here, Big Academia won't come here, and Big Business won't come here either. Let's do something different and unique.
